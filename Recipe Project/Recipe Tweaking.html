Pan Seared Sirlion Steak

This Sirloin Steak recipe can be made right at your stovetop at home.
It's tender, juicy and easy to make for an elegant weeknight dinner.
Serve with sauteed potatoes and green vegetables for the perfect meal.

Nutritional Information

Total Fat 14 grams
Saturated Fat 6 grams
Cholestorol 84 milligrams
Sodium 65 milligrams
Potassium 386 milligrams
Protein 24 grams

Ingredients

  • 2 sirloin steaks - 250g each, about 2.5cm thick
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • olive oil - 1 tbsp
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter - melted
  • 2 cloves garlic - minced
  • fresh thyme or rosemary - garnish - optional

Directions

  1. Pat down the meat to dry the steaks and season both sides with salt and pepper. Have all your other ingredients prepared and set aside a wire cooling rack.
  2. Place a large cast iron skillet over high heat and allow to heat up for one minute. Make sure your ventilation/exhaust fan is on!
  3. Add the olive oil and wait until it's shimmering before adding the steak. This is necessary to get a good sear.
  4. Fry for 2 minutes and then flip over. Fry on the other side for 2 more minutes.
  5. Pour the butter on top of the steak and scatter the minced garlic around.
  6. Cook one more minute until garlic is starting to turn brown on the edges.
  7. Remove steaks to a cooling rack and cover lightly with aluminum foil to keep warm.
  8. Let rest for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with optional rosemary or thyme, serve and enjoy!
